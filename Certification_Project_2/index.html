MegaStar Chiranjeevi

The Man the Star the Legend

The start of time line for Chiranjeevi's professional career:


  • 1978 - Chiranjeevi started his film career with Punadhirallu. However, his first released film was Pranam Khareedu

  • 1982 - He starred in Subhalekha, directed by K. Viswanath, which dealt with the social malady of the dowry system. It brought him his first Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu

  • 1983 - Released on 28 October 1983, the film, Khaidi was a massive commercial success and Chiranjeevi rose to stardom.

  • 1987 - Swayamkrushi chronicles the life of a self-educated cobbler's journey from rags to riches. The film was screened at the International Film Festival of India, the Asia Pacific Film Festival; the film was dubbed into Russian and was screened at the special mention section at the Moscow International Film Festival. Chiranjeevi garnered the Indian Express Best Actor, and the Nandi Award for Best Actor award for his performance.

  • 1988 - Chiranjeevi co-produced and acted in Rudraveena, which won the Nargis Dutt Award for Best Feature Film on National Integration, and the Nandi Special Jury Award.

  • 1990 - Jagadeka Veerudu Athiloka Sundari was theatrically released on May 9, 1990. The film upon release was a massive success, grossing ₹15 crore at the box office and becoming the highest grossing Telugu film in history at the time.

  • 1992 - Released on 9 April 1992, Gharana Mogudu became the first Telugu film to collect over ₹10 crore distributor share at the box office. The film made Chiranjeevi the highest-paid actor in India, with the English-language weekly magazine The Week describing him as "Bigger Than Bachchan" and gave him the moniker MegaStar

  • 2002 - Released on 24 July 2002, Indra was a major commercial success. Grossing more than ₹50cr on a budget of ₹10 crore, the film ended up becoming the highest-grossing Telugu film in history at the time.

  • 2008 - Chiranjeevi started Praja Rajyam Party, a political party in the state of Andhra Pradesh. In the 2009 general elections, the party won 18 of the 294 seats in the Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly.

  • 2011-12 - On 6 February 2011, Chiranjeevi merged the 30-month-old Praja Rajyam Party with the Indian National Congress. He got elected and took the oath as a Rajya Sabha member on 3 April 2012. On 28 October 2012, he was sworn in as the Union Minister of State (Independent charge), Ministry of Tourism, Government of India.

  • 2017 - Khaidi No. 150 marks Chiranjeevi's return to films in a lead role after a decade, also his 150th film. It became the second highest-grossing Telugu film of 2017, grossing over ₹164 crore (US$21 million) worldwide.

  • 2023 - Waltair Veerayya is Chiranjeevi's latest outing as the titular character.The film grossed ₹49.10 crore worldwide on its opening day.
